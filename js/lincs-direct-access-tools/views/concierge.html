The LINCS program

LINCS aims to create a network-based understanding of biology by cataloging changes in gene expression and other cellular processes after chemical perturbations. By systematically collecting and analyzing such data, LINCS will provide the biological research community with a reference dataset that is only achievable through large-scale efforts.

The LINCS data resource will be broadly applicable to both basic and translational research. For example, computational methods which integrate diverse data into a comprehensive view of normal and disease states can be applied for the development of new biomarkers and therapeutics.

By generating data on how cells respond to chemical perturbations and making it publicly available, the LINCS project will help us gain a more detailed understanding of cellular pathways and aid the development of new therapies that target diseased cell states.

LINCS: Library of Integrated Network-based Cellular Signatures

Data cubes

The combinatorial possibilities of cell lines, perturbations, and experimental platforms are viewed as data cubes. The LINCS project generates and analyses data in this form thus systematically filling out the space of experimental possibilities.

Data and signature generation centers

LINCS data are generated at 6 research centers each contributing complementary data cubes consisting of cell lines, perturbations, and assays. The centers have their own research goals and scientific priorities which are here outlined.

How to find data on the effects of small-molecule perturbations?

The LINCS project encompass a diverse and large-scale collection of data with relevance to many small-molecule compounds. There are many different search tools which can be used for querying such data. Each search tool has a distinct scope ranging from individual data sets, data from particular centers, all LINCS data, and incorporation of external data.

One query example is to find data sets which are perturbed with the compound Ruxolitinib.

How to find data on experiments where the gene expression of biological models have been altered?

Gene knockout through RNAi screens, overexpression through cDNA screens, or alterations through CRISPR are important methods for investigating cellular function. Many such genetic alterations are employed in the LINCS project but they are less numerous than small-molecule perturbations.

How to find data generated using particular experimental platforms?

The diversity of data types generated across the LINCS project reflect the increasing complexity of our understanding of biological processes. In this context it is important to be able to use and integrate data generated from different experimental platforms. Finding such data is the first step.

For general experimental approaches try queries such as Viability or Phosphorylation. If you know the LINCS platform name searching for L1000 or P100 ensures that you retrieved all such entries.

How to visually navigate and interpret LINCS data?

Data exploration is important for interpreting data where hypotheses are not yet established. By providing the community with a range of visualization tools applied to the LINCS data the project aims to facilitate effective exploration of the data generated.

Specialized visualizations for investigating particular aspects of LINCS data

No single visualization is likely to be informative for all LINCS data. Rather, different types of visualizations are useful for different data types and for different analysis contexts.


How to access LINCS data programmatically?

Establishing useful interfaces for computational biologists and computer scientists is a primary goal of the LINCS project.


How to download LINCS data?

As LINCS data is generated, the LINCS Data Portal curates, standardizes, and makes the data available for download. However, it is an open research question to what extent the diverse data can be meaningfully aggregated in this manner. For this reason, dedicated download tools could be more appropriate.
