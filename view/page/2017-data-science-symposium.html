BD2K-LINCS Data Science Symposium | May 16-18, 2017 | Cincinnati, OH

Systems Biology of Cellular Perturbations

The BD2K-LINCS Data Coordination and Integration Center (DCIC) held the second annual BD2K-LINCS Data Science Symposium (DSS 2017) on May 16-18, 2017.

The symposium brought together data scientists and translational scientists from academia, industry and government to discuss uses of big data in biomedicine. The general theme of the symposium was the systems biology of perturbation signatures and applications in drug development, translational biomedicine and environmental health. Talks addressed a range of issues related to leveraging Big Data in translational research including FAIR data principles and the emerging NIH Big Data ecosystem. The program also provided a survey of data science research pertaining to BD2K (Big Data to Knowledge) and LINCS (Library of Network-Based Cellular Signatures) consortia.

The symposium featured invited talks, poster session, and selected poster presentation sessions. A special session on Big Data in Environmental Health was held in the afternoon of May 16. For the list of invited speakers please refer to the Agenda page.

Funding

The symposium was organized by the BD2K-LINCS DCIC, the U54 center is jointly funded by NIH BD2K and LINCS programs (U54HL127624). The objective of the DCIC is to apply state of the art data science principles and methods in stewarding, integrating and analyzing data generated by the LINCS project. The session on Big Data in Environmental Health was co-sponsored by the Center for Environmental Genetics (ES06096).
