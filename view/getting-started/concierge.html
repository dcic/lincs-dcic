The LINCS program

LINCS aims to create a network-based understanding of biology by cataloging changes in gene expression and other cellular processes after chemical and genetic perturbations.

Computational methods which integrate diverse data into a comprehensive view of normal and disease states can be applied for the development of new biomarkers and therapeutics.

By generating and making public data on how cells respond to genetic and environmental stressors, the LINCS project will help us gain a more detailed understanding of cellular pathways and aid the development of new therapies that target diseased cell states.

Data cubes

The combinatorial possibilities of cell lines, perturbations, and experimental platforms are viewed as data cubes. The LINCS project generates and analyses data in this form thus systematically filling out the space of experimental possibilities.

How to find data on the effects of small-molecule perturbations?

The LINCS project encompass a diverse and large-scale collection of data with relevance to many small-molecule compounds. There are many different search tools which can be used for querying such data. Each search tool has a distinct scope ranging from individual data sets, data from particular centers, all lincs data, and external data.

One query example is to find data sets which are perturbed with the compound Ruxolitinib.

How to find data on experiments where the genetics of biological models have been altered?

Gene knockout through RNAi screens, overexpression through cDNA screens, or alterations through CRISPR are important methods for investigating cellular function. Many such genetic alterations are employed in the LINCS project but they are less numerous than small-molecule perturbations and for this reason harder to find.

How to find data generated using particular experimental platforms?

The diversity of data types generated across the LINCS project reflect the increasing complexity of our understanding of biological processes. In this context it is important to be able to use and integrate data generated from different experimental platforms. Finding such data is the first step.

For general experimental approaches try queries such as Viability or Phosphorylation. If you know the LINCS platform name searching for L1000 or P100 ensures that you retrieved all such entries.
